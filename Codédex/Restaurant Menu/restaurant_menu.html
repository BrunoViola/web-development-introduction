Spaghetti Carbonara

A Roman classic, Spaghetti Carbonara is made with pasta, eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, pancetta, and black pepper. The creamy sauce is created by combining the eggs and cheese with the heat from the pasta, resulting in a rich, flavorful dish. Traditionally, no cream is used, relying solely on the quality of the ingredients.


Risotto alla Milanese

A creamy rice dish from Milan, Risotto alla Milanese is flavored with saffron, giving it a distinct yellow hue. The rich taste is achieved by cooking the rice slowly in broth, with butter and Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese added for extra depth. This luxurious dish pairs beautifully with meat dishes like ossobuco.


Osso Buco

A traditional Milanese dish, Osso Buco features braised veal shanks cooked slowly with white wine, onions, carrots, celery, and a flavorful broth. The meat becomes tender and falls off the bone, with the bone marrow adding a rich, buttery taste. It is often served with a side of risotto or polenta, making it a comforting and elegant meal.
